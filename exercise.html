Catasorphizing: How to Stop Making Yourself Depressed and Anxious: Cognitive Distortion Skill #6

Catastrophizing is a cognitive distortion where we imagine the worst possible outcomes, leading to anxiety and depression. It closes us off to opportunities, invites the problems we fear, and makes us hopeless and unmotivated. To stop catastrophizing, we can start by getting a good night's rest, accepting uncertainty, and motivating ourselves with positive goals. The CBT approach involves noticing, challenging, and replacing catastrophizing thoughts with more rational ones.

Depression and anxiety: Exercise eases symptoms

The article discusses the relationship between exercise and the alleviation of symptoms associated with depression and anxiety. Despite initial reluctance, engaging in regular physical activity can significantly improve mood and reduce anxiety levels. Exercise not only benefits physical health by preventing and improving various conditions but also has profound effects on mental well-being.

Regular exercise releases endorphins, which are natural chemicals that promote a sense of well-being and help distract from negative thoughts. Moreover, achieving exercise goals can boost confidence and provide opportunities for social interaction, further enhancing mood. The article emphasizes that structured exercise programs are not the only option; any form of physical activity, such as walking or gardening, can be beneficial.

Mayo Clinic Staff. (2023, December 23). Depression and anxiety: Exercise eases symptoms. Retrieved from: https://www.mayoclinic.org/diseases-conditions/depression/in-depth/depression-and-exercise/art-20046495

Daily Routine To Fight Off Depression

Maintaining mental health requires daily actions, especially during stressful times like the pandemic. A routine that includes morning prayer or meditation, avoiding social media in the morning, getting dressed, taking vitamins, exercising, spending time in nature, practicing gratitude, and having social interactions can help prevent depression and promote mental well-being.

11 Tips for Coping with an Anxiety Disorder

The article titled "11 Tips for Coping with an Anxiety Disorder," authored by Siri Kabrick, APRN, C.N.P., provides valuable insights into managing anxiety disorders. It begins by distinguishing occasional feelings of anxiety from anxiety disorders, which involve frequent and excessive anxiety, fear, and panic. The symptoms of anxiety disorders are outlined, including nervousness, increased heart rate, and obsessive thoughts about triggers.

The article emphasizes the importance of seeking professional help if anxiety significantly impacts daily life and relationships. While psychotherapy and medications are often necessary treatments, lifestyle changes and coping strategies can also be effective. The 11 tips provided include maintaining physical activity, avoiding alcohol and drugs, managing stress through relaxation techniques, prioritizing sleep, and adopting a healthy diet.

Furthermore, the article encourages individuals to learn about their disorder, stick to treatment plans, identify triggers, keep a journal, and maintain social connections. It underscores the significance of seeking help early to prevent the worsening of anxiety symptoms.

Kabrick, S. (2021, June 20). 11 tips for coping with an anxiety disorder. Retrieved from https://www.mayoclinichealthsystem.org/hometown-health/speaking-of-health/11-tips-for-coping-with-an-anxiety-disorder
